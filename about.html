Yousif Sabba

My story begins when I first stepped into this school. The hall was packed with students, and it felt overwhelming and a bit sad. The classrooms are quite unique. As an old-school student, I'm not used to such modern designs. However, some areas could use a bit of improvement. When it comes to teachers, some are really nice, while others are quite unbearable. I think all teachers need to be a bit strict in how they talk to students. Students should respect teachers in the classroom. The way most students talk and walk around irritates the teachers, which is why they often seem angry.

Enough about the negatives, let's talk about something positive. Subjects are the main focus in any school. At NCST, they offer subjects that help students improve in their majors. There are diverse types of subjects. Let's break them into two parts: "Science" and "Technology." Science focuses on Engineering and Mechanics, which are more practical and hands-on. On the other hand, Technology covers AI, Cybersecurity, and Cloud Computing. I'm majoring in Cloud Computing, but that doesn't mean I'm unfamiliar with the other majors. Cloud Computing is interesting, but NCST makes it a bit challenging. The way students perceive information from the teachers involves a lot of knowledge to understand and demonstrate. Teachers should focus on providing clear, structured lessons that balance theory with practical applications, especially for technical fields like Cloud Computing. Apart from academics, NCST has a great environment for growth. The various extracurricular activities, workshops, and events offer students a chance to explore their interests and build essential skills. These opportunities, combined with a supportive peer network, make the learning experience more engaging and enriching. However, there’s always room for improvement, especially in how teaching methods align with industry standards and the evolving tech landscape. Teachers can play a pivotal role in making complex subjects more approachable and fostering a passion for learning among students. In conclusion, NCST is a place full of potential and promise, but like any institution, it has its challenges. With a few adjustments in teaching approaches and classroom management, it could truly shine as a beacon for future tech leaders.
That's it, Yousif Sabba
